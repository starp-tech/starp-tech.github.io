Starp: Decentralized Global ISP Network

Building Super Tolerant Networks Without a Governing Body

Presented by: Danila Puzikov

Market Opportunity

  • ISP stands for Internet Service Provider
  • The global ISP market is valued at over 400 billion
    and is growing 15% every year.
  • Increasing demand for reliable, decentralized network solutions.
  • Untapped potential in rural and underserved regions globally.

Problem Statement

  • Centralized control leads to outages, inefficiencies, and high costs.
  • Limited reach in rural and underserved areas.
  • Vulnerable to censorship and regulatory restrictions.
  • Cell equipment manfacturers monopoly on standards (GSM/LTE/5G).
  • High costs and limitations of mobile ISP and ROAMING

Solution Overview

Introducing Starp:

  • A decentralized ISP system enabling users to build super tolerant networks.
  • Open standard of cell communication proven to be harmless.
  • Eliminates the need for a central governing body.
  • Empowers individuals and communities in rural areas with information and communication technologies.

How It Works

  • Users become nodes by sharing their device's connectivity.
  • Each connection helps strengthen the network and earns the user mining rewards.
  • A mobile server enables mobile applications to participate in mining, expanding the network's reach and capacity.

Key Technologies and Security

  • Evolu.dev Offline Framework: Utilizes SQLite replication for data consistency and availability, even offline.
    Used in: Google Drive for offline access and synchronization.
  • OpenSSL RSA and AES-CBR: Ensures secure data transmission and storage with robust encryption protocols.
    Used in: Signal and WhatsApp.
  • WebTorrent and WebRTC: Facilitates efficient peer-to-peer data transfer and real-time communication.
    Used in: Netflix and Google Meet.
  • CoTURN Relays: Enhances connectivity and network reliability.
    Used in: Zoom.
